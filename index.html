Dodge Z Ball

Unreal Engine 4 C++ and BP | Aug 1, 2020 - Aug 8, 2020

Dodge Z Ball, Is Arena based, first-person action game focusing on survival mode style game play, where you must manipulate a single Dodgeball to destroy your enemies and survive the dodgeball onslaught.

Responsibilities

- Implement responsive ball throwing through instant-feedback UI and interpolated ball movement
- Programmed dash mechanic through C++, ensuring dash position was always valid by capsule tracing in direction of movement.
- Collaborated with teammates across the world using Git and C++ to avoid merge conflicts.

PreviousNext

Insight

Dodge Z Ball is a horde style action game where you knockout AI controlled enemies using your dodge ball. This is the first project I completed using a combination of C++ and Blueprints. This game helped me realize the importance of both C++ and Blueprints and why they coexist. Specifically while using Git for source control and having a team of programmers, using a mix of C++ and Blueprints was almost a necessity. As I soon found out, Git and binary files do not merge well together. This meant that only one person could work on a Blueprint actor at a time without losing data. In addition, Blueprints quickly got messy and took up a lot of screen space compared to traditional code. Using only Blueprints was not an option.

My time on the project was primarily focused on player gameplay and mechanics. I developed responsive dodge balls that can pierce and bounce off of enemies based on its state. In addition, I developed a dash mechanic that felt responsive, smooth, and prevented the player from getting stuck in walls through the use of capsule tracing. Finally, I worked on UI and design to polish off the game. I developed a UI system that works with the player mechanics and gives immediate feedback based on the state of the players dodge ball.

Hot Bots

Game Maker Studio GML | 2018 - 2019

Inspired by games alike, Robot Killing Game puts the user and 3 other teammates in an endless round of slaughter. Put against the odds, the player must survive and scavenge the battlefield for new items and resources to fend off the incoming waves of robots. Unlock new maps, weapons, skins, and classes as you level up and fight your way to the top. There is no end to the madness. New types of enemies appear as you battle.

Responsibilities

- Implement grid-based state machine AI that destroyed barriers and avoided obstacles
- Programmed AI that would actively strafe-dodge to avoid bullets or projectiles
- Develop proof-of-concept multiplayer system using TCP protocol through a server/client infrastructure.
- Implement data-based weapon system to rapidly implement new guns to the arsenal.
- Introduce experimental port to HTML5 to enable gameplay over the browser. 

PreviousNext

Insight

Hot Bots is a collection of ideas using the Game Maker Studio engine. I had created many prototypes prior but none had come close to the quality and ambition of this game. It could be that same reason that lead to its eventual downfall. Featuring a variety of weapons, fight against a horde of robot killing machines that will gradually get more difficult the longer you survive. Hot Bots was a dream project I developed during my high school years. While it was never finished, I experimented with many tools I never thought I would learn.

During one of the projects phases, it was possible to host and connect to other players using a server-client TCP connection. Enemies, weapons, bullets, and destructible walls would sync over the network seamlessly. In addition, interpolated movement was implemented to fill in for lost or delayed packets. Enemies also featured dodge mechanics that would actively try to dodge the players projectiles and special abilities to attack the player with.

Sticky Finger Sneak

Unreal Engine 4 C++ and BP | August 26th 2021- September 2nd 2021

Play as a master thief in Sticky Finger Sneak. Gather items without getting caught by the guards. Move valuables to your stash and get as much money as you can. Manage your inventory space and use each slot efficiently.

Responsibilities

- Implement balanced artificial intelligence that could chase, search, and alert nearby guards of the players presence
- Design and Program grid-based inventory system. The space an item takes in the players backpack will vary depending on size and orientation
- Implement lockdown state. Enemies will trigger a lockdown whenever the player is detected, closing all doors and requiring the player to reset the system.

PreviousNext

Insight

    After my short break working on personal projects, I decided to participate in another jam. My goal when joining the game jam was to continue improving my AI skillset. I took advantage of Unreal Engine's Behavior Tree system and the Environment Query System. I had experience using both systems in The Last Gasp. My main challenge was balancing and displaying useful information to the player. Similar to various stealth games, enemies had to be predictable and have their intentions clear. This was solved by having a visual status of the enemy and designing clear action/reaction artificial intelligence. 
    Alongside programming artificial intelligence, I also developed the inventory system for the project. The system uses a simple UDataAsset to store item data, the main problem was displaying and handling objects in the players inventory. By visualizing the inventory as a 2D grid of slots, the problem was broken apart and handled in parts. Internally, each slot was either taken or not. When an item was placed in the inventory, it would mark the slot as taken. To take or move an item, each item would store which slots it took up. With the backend finished, visualizing the inventory system to the player was straightforward.
